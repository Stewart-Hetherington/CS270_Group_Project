How to combat Anxiety

  1. Write a list of what you know is true
    • To counter what anxiety is telling you, keep a list of things you know are true and possible
    • For example, "I can handle going to the shop, I have done it before", etc.

  2. Read this list 2 - 3 times a day
    • Once you are aware of what is true, you will feel less afraid once anxiety comes
    • Anxiety makes us "forget what we know" which makes us vulnerable to it.
    • Having the knowledge and safety you need helps to combat anxious thoughts

  3. Try yoga or meditation, or exercise in general
    • These activities help distract the mind from negativity
    • Additionally they open up the mind to positive thinking and emotion
    • Physical exercise also helps the mind and body through hormones being released
    • Walking with a friend is a good way to connect with another person and deal with anxiety

  4. Pinpoint stress areas
    • Try to pinpoint the parts of your life that give you the most stress
    • Then devise possible ways of dealing with them positively
    • This gives you a goal or set of goals to work towards
    • Ultimately dealing with stress areas will lessen the impact of anxiety greatly

  5. Spend time with others
    • Chatting about your favourite hobbies or subjects to friends is a great way to bond and also relieve anxiety
    • Talking about your concerns to others you trust can help you understand your fears are not unusual and can be dealt with
    • Laughter is one of the best medicines

  6. Avoid procrastination if possible
    • Putting off things you need to do causes stress and anxiety to buildup
    • By completing tasks and chores, you will feel a sense of accomplishment
    • Doing simple things, such as sweeping or organising things, can help relieve stress and anxiety

  7. Try creating a routine
    • Getting into the groove of making sure you clean yourself, your surroundings, doing the tasks you need to do, etc., prevents a build up of things you need to do
    • This helps reduce the feeling of being overwhelmed by things and thus reduces anxiety
    • Routines help in giving you the confidence and control you need to feel strong to deal with your fears

  8. Practice dealing with negative thoughts
    • Challenge negative thinking
    • Re-associate your mind with positive affirmations, even when you are not experiencing panic or anxiety
    • Look at things in multiple perspectives
    • Always remind yourself that your brain will make things out to be worse than they are
